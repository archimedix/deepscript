# Party of Action and Solidarity

**Partidul Acțiune și Solidaritate**

## Basic Information
- **Founded**: May 15, 2016
- **Founder**: Maia Sandu
- **Headquarters**: Chisinau, Moldova
- **Ideology**: Pro-European, anti-corruption, liberal democratic
- **Political Position**: Center-right
- **Members**: ~100,000+ (approximate)
- **Political Status**: Active major political party

## Key Leadership
- **President**: Igor Grosu (2022-present)
- **President of Parliament**: Igor Grosu
- **Prime Minister**: Natalia Gavrilița (2021-2023), Vlad Recean (2023-2025)
- **President of Moldova**: Maia Sandu (2020-2024)

## Electoral Performance
- **2019**: 15 seats in Parliament (26.84% of votes)
- **2021**: 63 seats in Parliament (52.80% of votes)
- **2025**: 55 seats in Parliament (50.20% of votes)

## Political Position
- Strong pro-European integration stance
- Commitment to EU membership
- Anti-corruption platform
- Pro-Western foreign policy
- Maintains a "civic Moldovan nation" identity concept

## Significance
The Party of Action and Solidarity has been the dominant political force in Moldova since 2019, implementing European reforms, anti-corruption measures, and maintaining close ties with the European Union. The party has significantly shifted Moldovan politics, moving the country away from Russian influence towards a pro-Western orientation.

The party is central to Moldova's current political system, leading the country's democratic, European integration, and economic restructuring efforts.

## Related Entities
- Co-founding member of the "Now!" coalition (ACUM) in 2019
- Key player in Moldova's EU integration process
- Major political opposition to the Socialist Party of Moldova

## Notes
The party is notable for its "civic Moldovan nation" identity concept, proposed by founder Maia Sandu, which aims to transcend ethnic divisions to build a unified national identity while maintaining strong pro-European orientation.
