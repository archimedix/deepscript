## L'Office of Special Plans

### Creazione e Scopo

| Aspetto | Dettaglio |
|---------|-----------|
| **Creato** | Agosto 2002 |
| **Creatore** | Douglas Feith |
| **Ubicazione** | Pentagono |
| **Staff** | 16 persone circa |
| **Scopo dichiarato** | Analisi intelligence alternativa |
| **Scopo reale** | Bypassare CIA, costruire caso per guerra |

### Il "Cherry-Picking" dell'Intelligence

> "They picked out the ones that supported the case for going to war, and they discarded all the rest."
> — Robert Dreyfuss, giornalista

| Metodo | Descrizione |
|--------|-------------|
| **Selezione** | Scegliere dati che supportavano guerra, scartare il resto |
| **Fonti dubbie** | Curveball, Ahmed Chalabi |
| **Bypass CIA** | Presentare intelligence direttamente ai decisori |
| **Incontro Praga** | Promuovere teoria Atta-Iraq mai confermata |

### Rapporto Inspector General (Febbraio 2007)

Il Pentagon Inspector General concluse:

> "Developed, produced, and then disseminated alternative intelligence assessments on the Iraq and al Qaida relationship, which included some conclusions that were inconsistent with the consensus of the Intelligence Community."

| Conclusione | Dettaglio |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Azione** | Inappropriata |
| **Legalita'** | Non illegale (tecnicamente) |
| **Effetto** | Manipolazione intelligence per guerra |

### Reazione di Carl Levin

> "The bottom line is that intelligence relating to the Iraq–al-Qaeda relationship was manipulated by high-ranking officials in the Department of Defense to support the administration's decision to invade Iraq. The inspector general's report is a devastating condemnation."
> — Sen. Carl Levin, Chair Senate Armed Services Committee
