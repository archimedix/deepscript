## Controversia Warren

### La Critica

Sen. Elizabeth Warren (D-MA) chiese pubblicamente le dimissioni di Gottlieb dal board Pfizer:

> "This kind of revolving door influence-peddling smacks of corruption, and makes the American people rightly cynical and distrustful about whether high-level Trump administration officials are working for them, or for their future corporate employers."

### I Punti di Warren

| Critica | Dettaglio |
|---------|-----------|
| **Timing** | 83 giorni da regolatore a regolato |
| **Conflitto** | Pfizer ha "billions at stake" nelle decisioni FDA |
| **Accesso** | Pfizer "purchasing access" via Gottlieb |
| **Pattern** | Non caso isolato ma sistemico |

### La Risposta di Gottlieb

> "I worked with some large drugmakers before I came into the agency. I made no bones about the fact that I had expertise in life sciences and I made my living trying to promote innovation in this sector prior to coming to the agency."

### Proposta Legislativa Warren

**Anti-Corruption and Public Integrity Act**: divieto 4 anni per senior officials di lavorare per aziende regolate.

## Pattern Identificati

### 1. Revolving Door Classico

```
FDA Deputy (2005-07)
      |
      v
PRIVATE SECTOR (2007-17)
  - AEI (think tank conservatore)
  - NEA (venture capital)
      |
      v
FDA COMMISSIONER (2017-19)
      |
      v (83 giorni)
PFIZER BOARD (2019-)
  + Illumina Chairman
  + Multiple biotech boards
```

### 2. VC → Regolatore → VC + Boards

Gottlieb era gia' partner venture capital (NEA) prima di diventare Commissioner. Dopo FDA, torna a NEA E entra in board delle aziende regolate.

### 3. Think Tank come "Parcheggio"

AEI funge da posizione intermedia tra ruoli pubblici e privati - legittimazione intellettuale del revolving door.
