### Bank of America (2004-oggi)

**2004**: Bank of America acquisisce FleetBoston per $48 miliardi. Moynihan è tra i pochi executive FleetBoston a sopravvivere.

| Anno | Evento |
|------|--------|
| **2004** | Entra in BofA come President Global Wealth Management |
| **2008** | CEO Merrill Lynch post-acquisizione (sostituisce John Thain) |
| **2009** | President Global Banking & Wealth Management |
| **2010** | **CEO Bank of America** (succede a Kenneth Lewis) |
| **2014** | **Chairman of the Board** |
| **2020** | CEO of the Year (Chief Executive magazine) |

**Performance sotto Moynihan:**
- 215,000 dipendenti
- 68 milioni clienti consumer/small business
- 35 paesi
- "World's Best Bank" (Euromoney, 2x negli ultimi 5 anni)

## Pattern di Potere

### 1. Acquisitions Survivor

Sopravvissuto a due mega-acquisizioni:
- FleetBoston → Bank of America (2004)
- Merrill Lynch → Bank of America (2008)

In entrambi i casi, è emerso con ruoli maggiori.

### 2. Stakeholder Capitalism Architect

- Chair WEF International Business Council
- Co-autore Stakeholder Capitalism Metrics (con Schwab)
- Chair Sustainable Markets Initiative
- Guardian Council for Inclusive Capitalism

### 3. Banking-Policy Nexus

Simultaneamente:
- CEO seconda banca USA
- Chair Bank Policy Institute (lobbying)
- Federal Advisory Council (Fed)
- Business Roundtable

### 4. Irish-Catholic American Elite

Background working-class irlandese → vertice finanza USA. Chancellor Brown University consolida status establishment.
