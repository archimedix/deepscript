# Petróleos de Venezuela S.A. (PDVSA)

> State oil company with world's largest reserves, symbol of Venezuela's rise and collapse

## Executive Summary

| Metrica | Valore |
|---------|--------|
| **Fondazione** | 1 January 1976 |
| **Sede** | Caracas, Venezuela |
| **Tipo** | Company (Oil & Gas) |
| **Leadership** | Gustavo González López (President), Delcy Rodríguez (Oil Minister) |
| **Riserve** | 303 billion barrels (world's largest) |
| **Produzione** | ~934K bpd (2024) |
| **Status** | active (crisis) |

## Glossario

| Termine | Definizione |
|---------|-------------|
| Orinoco Belt | Vast heavy crude deposit in eastern Venezuela |
| SEBIN | Servicio Bolivariano de Inteligencia Nacional (intelligence service) |
| Petrocaribe | Venezuelan oil diplomacy program (2005-) |
| Heavy crude | Dense, viscous oil requiring upgrading before refining |

---

## Fondazione e Storia

| Dato | Dettaglio |
|------|-----------|
| **Fondazione** | 1 January 1976 |
| **Contesto** | Nationalization under President Carlos Andrés Pérez |
| **Obiettivo dichiarato** | State control of oil industry amid 1970s oil boom |
| **Predecessors** | Creole Petroleum (Exxon), Shell Venezuela, Gulf, others |

### Timeline Storica

| Anno | Evento |
|------|--------|
| **1976** | Nationalization; PDVSA created from 14 operating companies |
| **1986** | Acquires 50% of Citgo (US refiner) |
| **1990** | Acquires remaining 50% of Citgo |
| **1998** | Peak production: 3.4M bpd. Hugo Chávez elected president |
| **2002-03** | Oil strike; 18,000 PDVSA workers fired |
| **2004** | Rafael Ramírez becomes president (until 2014) |
| **2007** | Forced nationalization of Orinoco Belt projects; ExxonMobil, ConocoPhillips exit |
| **2013** | Maduro takes power; production decline accelerates |
| **2014** | Oil price crash; Venezuelan economy collapses |
| **2017** | US sanctions begin targeting PDVSA |
| **2019** | Full US sanctions on PDVSA; production falls below 1M bpd |
| **2023** | Temporary sanctions relief under Biden |
| **2024** | CEO Tellechea removed/arrested; Obregón appointed |
| **2025** | Citgo sold to Amber Energy for $5.9B |

## US Sanctions

### Timeline

| Year | Action |
|------|--------|
| 2017 | Bond trading restrictions |
| 2019 | Full sanctions on PDVSA oil exports |
| 2023 | Temporary 6-month relief (Barbados agreement) |
| 2024 | Relief not renewed |

### Impact
- Blocked PDVSA from US financial system
- Prevented oil imports to US
- Accelerated production decline
- Citgo protected from creditor seizure (until 2025)

---

## Corruption and Mismanagement

### 2002-2003 Strike Aftermath
- 18,000+ workers fired (engineers, geologists, managers)
- Technical expertise gutted
- Political loyalty prioritized over competence

### Rafael Ramírez Era (2004-2014)
- Dual role: Oil Minister + PDVSA President
- "Atlantic case": ~$5B embezzlement alleged
- Fled Venezuela; extradition requested 2021

### Recent Arrests
- **Pedro Tellechea** (Oct 2024): Accused of selling info to CIA-linked company
- **Tareck El Aissami** (Mar 2023): Former Oil Minister, corruption charges
